Zoos and animal welfare

Zoological park

An establishment which maintains a collection of wild animals, typically in a park or gardens, for study, conservation, or display to the public.

Animal welfare

Opportunity to express species-typical behaviours to cope with environmental or internal stressors (Broom, 1980).

Effects of poor welfare

  • Loss of behaviour diversity.
  • Lowered fertility.
  • Increased juvenile mortality.
  • Unstable captive population.
  • Unfit for repatriation.

Introduction

Zoos are man made creations.Hence zoos provide a rare glimpse into the ever-changing relationship between nature and man. The objectives of a modern zoo goes beyond public recreation into the fields of conservation breeding and ex-situ conservation. - captivity forces animals to relinquish control over their environment. - Which leads to chronic stress resulting in a loss of behaviour diversity, lowered fertility, and other factors leading to a unstable captive population unfit of repatriation to the wilderness. - Animal welfare as a concept attempts to provide animals the opportunity to cope with internal and external stressor & thereby regain control which leads to better psychosomatic outcomes.

I already knew about the rearing history of the subjects. - To categorize subjects based on boldness or shyness, I took a two-pronged approach - first, I asked keepers with more than three years of experience to rate subjects on a scale of 1-9 on 15 personality attributes. - Then I introduced each subject separately to non-food novel objects and recorded their reaction - Independent observers recorded the latency times to react to novel objects and the type of behavior ( playful, fearful, curious) performed by the animal. - I created our own list of bold and shy animals and compared the same with keepers. - I only included individuals whose personality traits were agreed upon by keepers and observers.

Methods: Corticosterone validation

Chapter 2

We tested for prallelism and accuracy to validate the corticosterone assay. - For parallelism we pooled extracts from a random combination of samples to find optimal dilutions for final assays. - We plotted relative dose against the standard and a parallel slope indicates similar immunoreactivity to the standard solution. - For accuracy test we spiked corticosterone standards with different dilutions of the same faecal concentrate solution. and plotted the results using observed and expected concentrations to ensure that faecal contaminants did not interefere with the results. - to ensure reliability, We also calculated intra and inter assay coefficients of variation using repeated measures of same pooled extracts .

Study design

  • Subjects randomly divided into control(N =16, seven enclosures) and test group(N =19, seven enclosures).
  • Baseline period lasted 14 days with weekly scat collection.
  • Enrichment interventions were made for one week period.
  • 14 days of post-enrichment data collection commenced.

Chapter 3

  • I used 35 Asiatic lions housed at 14 enclisures for this study.
  • Enclosures were randomly assigned to control and test enclosures.
  • Baseline behaviour observation with weekly scat collection commenced for 14 days for both control and test groups.
  • After that I introduced a combined enrichment intervention at the test enclosures, while no changes were made to the control enclosures.
  • Following a one week habituation period. I collected post-treatment data on both control and test group subjects to measure the impacts of the enrichment interventions.

Enrichment is defined as any device, or management practice that is designed to provide captive animals

Chapter summary

  • Enrichment interventions led to positive impact on the behavioural and physiological welfare indices.
  • A universal welfare evaluation framework.
  • A combined enrichment approach of manipulable, feeding and sensory enrichment is best suited for long term engagement for Asiatic lions.

Chapter 3

  • Animal welfare not only means prevention of cruelty, it also seeks to create opportunities for captive animals to experience positive affective states.

  • Our study is the first to showcase the impact of enrichment interventions on the behavioural and physiological welfare of Asiatic lions conclusively.

  • The welfare measurement framework used in this study is largely species-agnostic and can be applied to any modern zoological institution to assess welfare impacts of goal-oriented enrichment interventions.

  • I found that enrichment devices specially manipulable and feed based enrichment create a positive relation with novel objects and lead to better welfare outcomes and longer engagement times. however there should be a large number of such objects to prevent infighting and aggressive monopolizations.

  • The enrichment interventions shown here require little monetary input but can have large positive welfare impacts. Therefore it is suggested to incorporate them in the management of large captive felids at Indian zoos and conservation breeding programmes.

Summary and recommendations

  • Animal welfare is affected by enclosure complexity.
  • Personality and rearing history cause differential welfare outcomes to similar management practices.
  • Bold and shy personality traits are ubiquitous across both rearing-history types.
  • Enclosure enrichment integrated with daily husbandry can lead to positive welfare outcomes.

Recommendations

  • Animal welfare should be the driver of captive animal management.
  • Both external and internal factors should be considered while measuring welfare in captive animals.
  • Inter-individual differences can lead to differential welfare outcomes to similar husbandry practices.
  • Enrichment interventions can improve welfare parameters significantly.
  • Lack of scientific inputs and manpower should be addressed.
  • Animal welfare is an overlooked aspect of captive animal management across most Indian zoos and conservation breeding centers.
  • My thesis shows that animal welfare is dependent on the enclosure complexity and it species appropriateness followed by internal factors like personality and rearing history.
  • Finally, Enrichment interventions are very effective in creating opportunities for good welfare and should be integrated with incumbent management practices.
  • I have visited several small medium and large zoos in India and one thing stands out. There is a distinct lack of manpower at most of them.
  • Zoo management need to invest in keeper training and zoo biologists to address the welfare epidemic at Indian zoos.
